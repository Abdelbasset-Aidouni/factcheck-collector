“Nirbhaya’s rapists had no access to sex hence they raped”, Nobel Laureate Abhijit Banerjee had claimed in 2012

by TFIPOST News Desk
15 October 2019
in Opinions
17.6k

Abhijit Banerjee, an Indian-American economist, won Nobel Prize in Economics (officially known as The Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel) along with Esther Duflo and Michal Kremer “for their experimental approach to alleviating global poverty”. He is second person of Indian descent and third person of non-European ancestry to be conferred with the prize.

Banerjee is a classic example of a left-liberal intellectual. He completed his studies from Presidency University, Kolkata and JNU, New Delhi. While in JNU, he was involved in student activism symbolic to JNU and was jailed in Tihar for 10 days. The entire left-liberal cabal went into an overdrive yesterday after Abhijit Banerjee, a vocal critic of the Modi government, was conferred the prize.

Pay to TFI

Abhijit Banerjee, like any other left-liberal intellectual, has made many substandard and absurd arguments. In October 2012, when anger against Delhi gang rape was at its peak, Banerjee argued that “inequality of access to sex” is among the reasons behind rapes. “There are few forces more powerful than sexual desire and few forms of inequality more palpable than inequality of access to sex,” wrote Banerjee in an article in Hindustan Times.

He shamelessly tried to justify the rape with inequality of access to sex. Tomorrow, he might justify theft, dacoity, and loot in the name of income inequality.

Going by Banerjee’s logic, one can also justify terrorism in the name of discrimination with minority community. Murder can be justified in the name of other person’s misdeeds. The Naxals can justify their heinous deeds in the name of government oppression. Mob violence can be justified in the name of provocation by the other person. Majoritarianism can be justified in the name of ‘maximum benefit to highest number of people’.

But one has to draw a line somewhere, otherwise everyone will have reasons to justify their deeds, and the of ‘law of jungle’ will prevail.

Abhijit Banerjee argued that the poor people in cities do not have access to sex and as a society we must do something, otherwise, rapes will not stop. “What are we doing as a society to reduce inequality of access to sex? I don’t mean publicly provided brothels — though those are not unknown in history — but just the right to a normal conjugal life,” he had asked.

He also gave example of how Khap Panchayats prioritize the welfare of society over individual welfare. “When the khap panchayats talk about getting everyone married at 16, they want to “solve” the problem of sexual desire (not how they put it, but that’s what they are talking about), as they always have, by putting “society’s” interests above that of young women (and men). But that does not mean we do not have a problem.”

This is a typical Marxist idea where the interest of community prevails over the interest of individual. Communism (Commue or Community) is based on this idea.

Abhijit Banerjee had also proposed the NYAY scheme of the Congress party. The NYAY scheme which had been propagated as the panacea for poverty alleviation had been proposed by the Indian-American economist, was severely rejected by the electorate during the Lok Sabha polls earlier this year.

The NYAY scheme was a disastrous idea that had been desperately proposed by the Congress in the run up to the Lok Sabha polls. The NYAY scheme of the Congress party would have cost around 3.6 lakh crore rupees to the exchequer or 14 percent of total government expenditure. It would have cost around 2 percent of GDP and the central government’s net collection is around 10 percent of GDP.

In an interview with Rahul Shivshankar of Times Now he categorically said, “No NYAY without tax increase.” In another interview with Rajeev Dubey of Business Today he said that there is scope for direct as well as indirect tax increase. When Banerjee was asked what taxes should increase and what are the new taxes that can be imposed, he said, “Income taxes. In principle, there’s scope for wealth taxation and in some sense there’s scope for increasing GST.”

One can imagine what kind of society we will have if Banerjee’s ideas are implemented. The ultimate goal of Banerjee is to establish a’ Communist Society’.
