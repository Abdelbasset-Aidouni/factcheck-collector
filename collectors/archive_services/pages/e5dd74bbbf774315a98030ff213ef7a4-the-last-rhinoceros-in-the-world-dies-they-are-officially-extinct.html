The last rhinoceros in the world dies. They are officially extinct

by Bill Lawler July 30, 2018, 10:04 am

The last black rhinoceros that remained in the earth died a few days ago when he was transferred to what would be his new home, an ecological reserve that protects endangered species.

The population of black rhinos decreased in recent years and was limited to two individuals in the world, in their attempt to protect them were moved to Nairobi, an incredible site for rhinos in Tsavo East, Kenya.

The specialists have said that during the transfer different negligences happened and that the necessary measures were not taken to protect them, they arrived very restless and completely dehydrated to the place.

“The first investigations indicate that the rhinoceros died of intensified stress and salt poisoning, since the animal had to adapt to the salty water of its new home,”  said the doctor who attended them in an emergency. 
The specialist from the University of Nairobi, said that the rhinoceroses were already almost dying, they could not adapt to their new home, especially because of the salty water of the park, which caused them poisoning.

The male rhinoceros died a few days after arriving at the park and, on the other hand, the female was attacked by a group of lions, although she is very serious and continues fighting for her life.

The internal director of the park was already fired with other officials after discovering the negligence, although of course, nothing can justify and compensate this horrible misfortune, the reproduction of the black rhinoceros has stopped forever.

So far this year different species have been declared extinct, for example the Addax antelope and the Irrawaddy river dolphin which surprises us and puts us on emergency alert. 
Undoubtedly this is a horrible news for nature, humans are taking charge of killing our animal species instead of protecting them with respect and affection.
