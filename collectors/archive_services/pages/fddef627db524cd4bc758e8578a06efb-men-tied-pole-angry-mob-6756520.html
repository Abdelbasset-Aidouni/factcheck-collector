Men tied to pole by angry mob after 'stealing, shooting and poisoning dogs'

WARNING: Contains distressing images. Police dispatched more than 100 officers to diffuse the angry mob who had gathered to shame the men

Two men were beaten up and publicly humiliated by an angry mob after they allegedly stole and killed two dogs.

The men were accused of shooting their neighbours' dogs with slingshots before poisoning them.

According to the Guiyang Evening News, a man called Mr Xiao said he caught the two men shooing his dogs in his backyard at 6am.

He shouted to two of his neighbours who chased the alleged thieves - who then fell into a trench while trying to flee on a motorbike.

They were caught by angry villagers who beat them up, tied them to an electricity pole and hung the dead dogs around their necks.

  • Read more: Angry wives attack mistresses in street in China after social media trend

Several police officers were called to the scene to calm the situation but were forced to call for back up when they angry mob got out of hand.

The incident happened in southwest China's Guizhou Province, where more than 100 government police officers were eventually dispatched to handle the situation.

By the time the accused men were freed, they had been held by villagers for 10 hours.

The alleged dog thieves have been detained by police who are investigating their reported beating.

  • Read more: Horrific video shows mob of villagers tie up PREGNANT woman and beat her

Chinese users on Weibo, a social networking site similar to Twitter, have posted angry comments about the two men.

One user, called @maimiaixiong, wrote: "The shame parade served them right; such people deserve no mercy."

Another posted: "The men deserve their punishment, although the villagers might have been a little extreme in their actions."

And one remarked: "It's wrong to steal a dog, but unlawful detention and demeaning pillories are even worse."
