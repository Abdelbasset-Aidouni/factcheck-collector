Ayodhya Deepotsav: UP govt plans to spend Rs 133 crore to light 5.5 lakh diyas

Times Travel Editor|TRAVEL TRENDS, AYODHYAOct 23, 2019, 16.18 IST


Ahead of Diwali, the Yogi Adityanath-led Uttar Pradesh government will be celebrating the much-publicised Deepotsav, also known as ‘the festival of lamps’ in Ayodhya on 26 October, 2019. On the occasion, more than 5.5 lakh diyas or earthen lamps will be lit, which will cost around INR 133 crore. It is stated that the UP government will bear all the expenses for the grand event. The decision was taken by the state cabinet recently.

Before this, the cost of organising Deepotsav at Ayodhya was taken care of by the UP Tourism Department from its own budget. However, this year the state government will be funding the function via district administration.

Srikant Sharma, a government spokesperson said that the Deepotsav that was being organised by the Tourism Department till now would, henceforth, be a state government affair. All the preparations are almost done and the government has made some great plans for the five-day-long festival. The festival of lights, Diwali also marks the return of Lord Ram to Ayodhya after 14 long years.

Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) leader Ved Prakash Gupta said, "Over Rs 130 crore has been allocated by the government for the preparations this time. The vision behind Deepotsav is not just spiritual, but also to boost tourism in the area". He also said that this is the best way to boost tourism in the state.

Last year, in 2018, a Guinness World Record was made in Ayodhya wherein more than three lakh earthen lamps were lit for 45 minutes on the banks of River Saryu.
