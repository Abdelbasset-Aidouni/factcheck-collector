24 days of Christmas: Unwrap an Aussie animal

20 Dec 2018

Keywords

  • Christmas
  • biodiversity

This holiday season, unwrap an Aussie animal every day with WWF-Australia's 24 days of Christmas!

 

Starting with...

 

On the 1st day of Christmas Australia sent to me… an emu up a gum tree!

On the 2nd day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a pink galah, almost wearing Santa Claus’ colours.

On the 3rd day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a kookaburra laughing!

On the 4th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a koala cuddling! 

On the 5th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... two kangaroos bouncing!

On the 6th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... brolgas dancing! 

On the 7th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... possums playing! 

On the 8th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... dingoes digging!  

On the 9th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a wombat working!  

On the 10th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a goanna leaping!  

On the 11th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a numbat nibbling!

On the 12th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a parrot prattling! 

On the 13th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... turtles tickling! 

On the 14th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a northern bettong nesting!

On the 15th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a weedy seadragon drifting! 

On the 16th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a Baudin’s black cockatoo caroling! 

On the 17th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a thorny devil dining!  

On the 18th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a Tassie devil darting! 

On the 19th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a crocodile cavorting! 

On the 20th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a superb fairy wren warbling! 

On the 21st day of Christmas Australia sent to me... an echidna enjoying the sunshine. 

On the 22nd day of Christmas Australia sent to me... snubfin dolphins diving!  

On the 23rd day of Christmas Australia sent to me... a flying fox frolicking!   

On the 24th day of Christmas Australia sent to me... two platypuses wishing you happy holidays!
